Vice Presidential Debate Analysis

October 7, 2020

I've come to realize that text analysis is probably the most frequent type of data analysis I perform, both professionally and personally. There are a few reasons why I think this has occurred:

  1. Data Availability: Text data is everywhere. It's in contracts, tweets, chat transcripts, websites, etc. There is an unlimited supply of text data.
  2. Creative Analysis: Unlike numeric data, where there are often standard analyses performed (e.g. mean, deviation, correlation, etc.), text data lends itself to creative intrepretation. You have to think creatively about the story you are wish to tell from the text and how to quantify that story. I enjoy the creative challenge of combining mathematics with free form text.
  3. Regex: Obtaining data from text elements is challenging, Fortunately there is regex. Regex gives you the power to obtain whatever any data element you wish from text, as long as you can identify the pattern. For example, in the Data Viz to the right, I used regex to grab:
    • - The time speakers started/stopped speaking
    • - To identify the speaker of a certain phrase
    Most casual data analysts are used to working with structured tables, and thus I find people often reach out to me to parse more complicated/unstructured text data.

About the Data Viz

The chart on the right showcases a custom data visualization built entirely in R. The chart illustrates the total amount of time each candidate spoke during the VP Debate as the night progressed. I particularly like the option in R to create an animated graph, since this data shows a process over-time.

Another great feature in R is the ability to create multiple graphs on the same page. The bar graph in the top left shows running time gap (hours: minutes) between the two speakers. This helps the viewer to better quantify the difference between the two lines.

How I built this visualizaiton

  1. Webscrape the transcript (source)
  2. Regex to parse out time stamps and speakers
  3. Custom data visualization
